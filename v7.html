Background

I have spent 13 years putting algorithmic decision-making into production. Seven of them were client-facing at AVEVA and Schneider Electric, where I went from Technical Officer to Global Solutions Architect and deployed optimisation systems into some of the world's largest mining operations. One integration I delivered for a Tier 1 miner cut load rejection from 9% to 2%. Since then it has been government platforms, an ISO 27001 implementation I led from scratch, and now AI agents in places where compliance is not optional.

Outside work hours I run mechanistic interpretability experiments with TransformerLens and sparse autoencoders, and I build agent tooling that I actually use every day.

How I work

Every engagement ends up following the same pattern. I sit with the people who actually do the work, map how it really gets done, design something practical, and then build it myself rather than handing a diagram to someone else.

With AI adoption I treat the technology as the easy part. The hard part is delivery. Trial in a small scope, measure it, write the playbook, train the team, then scale. If an auditor cannot trace it, it does not count.

Working with me

I help organisations adopt AI when compliance rules like ISO 27001 make that hard. In practice that means rollout architecture, cost models, governance guardrails, and a business case built from data instead of hype. I have done this for a 20-person engineering team on AWS Bedrock.

I have been around this kind of software for longer than LLMs have existed. Seven years of optimisation systems in mining taught me what production actually demands, and building my own AI projects keeps me honest about what is production-ready, what is theatre, and what I would never put in front of a client.

The easiest way to reach me is GitHub.
